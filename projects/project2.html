Fitmigo ‚Äì AI Personal Trainer

AI-powered fitness assistant that uses computer vision and deep learning to detect exercise repetitions, track form, and give real-time feedback.

Problem

Many people lack access to personal trainers, making it difficult to maintain proper form or measure progress. Existing fitness apps often only count reps without evaluating quality. The goal of Fitmigo was to create an accessible, computer-vision-based trainer that delivers both accuracy and feedback.

Approach

  • Pose Detection: Used MediaPipe for skeletal keypoint tracking across multiple joints.
  • Feature Engineering: Extracted joint angles and time-series motion vectors from pose data.
  • Neural Network Model: Trained a PyTorch classifier to detect exercise type and rep count (~95% accuracy across push-ups, squats, curls).
  • Form Evaluation: Applied rule-based thresholds on joint angles (e.g., squat depth, elbow extension) to grade form quality.
  • User Testing: ~150 live tests across different body types and lighting conditions.

Results

  • Accuracy: ~95% detection accuracy for rep counting across multiple exercises.
  • Robustness: Worked under varying environments (indoor/outdoor, low-light).
  • User Adoption: Tested by 30+ peers, who highlighted real-time form feedback as most valuable feature.

Reflection

This project sharpened my skills in computer vision, time-series classification, and user-centered design. It also taught me the importance of combining machine learning models with domain knowledge (biomechanics) for practical applications.
