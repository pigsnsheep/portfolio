Gene Expression & Cancer Drug Response

Research project analyzing gene expression data to predict cancer drug response. Published in a peer-reviewed journal, this work combined dimensionality reduction, clustering, and predictive modeling to identify biomarkers of treatment sensitivity.

Problem

Precision medicine depends on identifying which patients will respond to specific cancer drugs. Gene expression datasets are high-dimensional (20k+ genes) and noisy, making prediction challenging. The project tested the effectiveness of spectral clustering, a well-known dimensionality reduction technique relatively unexplored in the field of gene expression, against traditional models such as PCA, UMAP, and t-SNE.

Approach

  • Data: Downloaded and downsampled gene expression data form the Geneotype-Tissue Expression Project V9 Dataset.
  • Parameter Optimization: .
  • Benchmarking: Created PCA, UMAP, K-means, t-SNE models for comparison.
  • Evaluation: Used a aggregation of Silhouette, Calinski-Harabasz, and Davies-Bouldin scores to evaluate each model's effectivness at clustering the data.

Results

  • Discovered that spectral clustering performed significantly better than PCA and K-means while achieving similar scores to UMAP and t-SNE, while being twice as efficient.
